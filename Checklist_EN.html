How to get the most out of it

← Back to Jami setup

Communication

  • If you cannot attend, please let me know as soon as possible.
  • If you expect to be late or would like to reschedule, simply send a message.
  • If you have specific questions or topics you would like to discuss, feel free to let me know in advance.

Preparation

  • Please upload new problem sheets as soon as they become available.
  • Lecture notes, personal notes and textbook information are always helpful.
  • If possible, share relevant material in advance.
  • Mark exercises that you find particularly difficult.
  • Fully charge your laptop and if available, your headset.

During the session

  • Try to find a quiet working environment.
  • A stable internet connection helps both of us.
  • If you have access to a tablet with a stylus, feel free to use it for collaborative note-taking.
  • Keep paper and a pen nearby, even if we work digitally.

Ask questions

  • Questions are always welcome.
  • If something is unclear, please interrupt immediately.
  • Tutoring is not a lecture and not a performance; it is a collaborative learning process.
  • The earlier misunderstandings are identified, the more efficiently we can work.

Especially helpful

  • Try to develop your own ideas before getting into the session.
  • Keep a list of questions during the week.
  • Bring incomplete solution attempts as well; they are often very instructive.
  • If you discover an error, bring it along. We often learn more from mistakes than from finished solutions.
